Evaluation Task: Emotion Intensity Rating

For this task, you will listen to a generated audio sample. You will also be given a specific emotion label (Happy, Angry, Sad, or Surprised). Your task is to very carefully listen to the audio and rate how intense or activated that given emotion feels in the speaker's voice on a scale of 1 to 5.

Focus solely on the strength or perceived intensity of the specified emotion in the audio, regardless of whether other emotions might also be present or the speech content.

Intensity Scale:

  • 1: Very Low Intensity / Not Noticeable – You hear little to no trace of the specified emotion in the voice.
  • 2: Low Intensity / Slightly Noticeable – You detect a slight hint or minimal presence of the specified emotion.
  • 3: Moderate Intensity / Clearly Noticeable – The specified emotion is clearly present and discernible in the voice, but not overwhelming.
  • 4: High Intensity / Strong – The specified emotion feels strong and quite prominent in the voice.
  • 5: Very High Intensity / Extremely Strong – The specified emotion feels extremely strong, highly activated, or highly expressive in the voice.

Instructions:

  • Note the emotion label provided for the audio sample.
  • Listen to the generated audio sample with maximum attention.
  • Based only on the audio, rate the intensity or activation level of the specified emotion using the 1–5 scale provided above.
  • Select the single score (1, 2, 3, 4, or 5) that best represents the intensity of the specified emotion as you perceive it in the audio.
