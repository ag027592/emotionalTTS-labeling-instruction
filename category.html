Emotion Category Identification

Annotation Task: Identifying the Emotion

This task involves labeling the emotions conveyed in each speech sample. These labels correspond to fundamental human emotions that typically arise spontaneously and are associated with distinct physiological patterns and vocal expressions. You will be asked to listen to short speech clips and select the one emotion that you perceive as most prominent. Please review the descriptions below to better understand the emotional categories.

Emotion Categories:

  • Angry: Speech typically features high energy, a rapid speaking rate, and minimal pauses. The voice may sound sharp, tense, or intense.
    Common triggers: obstruction, injustice, betrayal, observing rule violations, or perceived harm to loved ones.
  • Sad: Characterized by low energy, slow speech, frequent pauses, and a soft or low-pitched voice. In some cases, it may manifest as louder or higher-pitched wailing.
    Common triggers: rejection, loss, disappointment, or endings.
  • Happy: Marked by high energy, fast speech, and vocal cues such as laughter, squeals of joy, excited shouts, or sighs of contentment.
    Common triggers: achievement, humor, acts of kindness, enjoyable environments, or emotional connection.
  • Surprise: Often brief, featuring a sudden gasp or pause that quickly transitions to the next vocal segment.
    Common triggers: unexpected sounds or movements.
  • Neutral: Characterized by steady energy, a moderate speaking rate, and minimal vocal variation. The voice is typically calm, even-toned, and controlled, without strong emotional inflection.
    Common triggers: routine conversation, objective narration.
  • Other: If none of the predefined categories match the emotion you perceive as primary, select Other and describe the emotion using your own words (e.g., amused, frustrated, annoyed, concerned, etc.).

Instruction:
For each audio clip, answer the following question:
Is one of the listed emotions the primary emotion expressed in this audio?
If yes, select the most appropriate emotion from the list.
If not, choose Other and provide a brief description of the emotion you perceive.
