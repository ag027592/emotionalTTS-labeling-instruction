Demo Description: Emotion Annotation Tasks

This demo presents examples to help annotators understand two types of labeling tasks related to emotional speech: Emotion Intensity Rating and Emotion Category Identification.

1. Emotion Intensity Rating

Annotators are asked to listen to each audio clip and rate how strongly a specific emotion (e.g., Happy, Angry, Sad, or Surprised) is expressed in the speaker’s voice. Ratings are made on a 5-point scale, from 1 (very low intensity) to 5 (very high intensity).

The table below organizes sample clips by emotion category, speaker gender, and intensity level to illustrate different levels of emotional activation.

2. Emotion Category Identification

In this task, annotators listen to a speech sample and determine which single emotion is most clearly expressed. Options include: Angry, Sad, Happy, Surprised, Neutral, and Other. If "Other" is selected, annotators are asked to briefly describe the perceived emotion in their own words.

The sample clips are grouped by emotion labels to demonstrate how different emotions typically sound in speech.

3. Speaker Age Group Annotation

This demo provides example audio clips to help annotators understand the speaker age group labeling task. Each audio sample corresponds to one of the following age groups: Child (4–12 years old), Teenager (13–19 years old), Adult (20–64 years old), or Elderly (65+ years old). Please listen carefully to each clip and select the single age group that best matches your perception of the speaker’s voice.
